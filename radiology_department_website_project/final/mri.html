MRI scan

An MRI (magnetic resonance imaging) scan provides an image similar to a CT scan but uses magnetism and radio waves to build up a series of cross sectional images. MRI pictures provide additional information on the characteristics of tissues and organs that is often not available from other techniques. For this reason MRI has potential to reduce the number of certain diagnostic procedures. MRI does not use X-rays and the magnetic fields are not known to be harmful. However, it takes longer to obtain the pictures than a conventional X-ray machine or CT.
